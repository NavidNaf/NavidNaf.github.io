An Excerpt from A Human Soul

Fiction • April 16, 2020 • 1 Min Read

I have always been a “complainer”, yet I never did anything, rather than complaining. My boundary was till the complaining, but when authority ever confronted me, I denied and became the person that supports the complaint. Why? I always wanted to be on the safe side. I complained and I still remained on the safe side.

I hated being the reason for chaos.

And most importantly, I wanted people to follow me, look up to me as the idol. Yet another God who can be worshiped.

I hated all the Gods, yet I wanted to establish me as God.

I hated my race, yet I wanted to create my own race.

I hated my race, yet I wanted to create my own race.

I hated my gender, yet I wanted to make my gender reign the world.

I hated being the reason for chaos, yet I was the chaos itself.

And I never understood me and I never will. I can’t be wrong, I am the divine. I am the start and I am the end. And I have the knowledge that no one will understand. I can complain, but I won’t fix, that lowers down my state. I am the messiah turned God, who complains about the existence of God, Race, Rules, Genders, and Chaos. I will not accept my faults, I will never fix myself. But I will carp, I will cavil, I will bleat, I will whine. Cause I am the Messiah turned God, who can’t be wrong.
