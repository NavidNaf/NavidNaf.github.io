Report a Bug to RedHawks

Bug reporting is essential to RedHawks Cyber Research Projects as it facilitates the identification and mitigation of vulnerabilities across a wide range of research endeavors. By reporting bugs encountered during investigations, our team can address weaknesses in systems, protocols, or methodologies. This proactive approach not only strengthens the integrity of our research findings but also contributes to advancing cybersecurity knowledge and practices, ultimately leading to more secure digital infrastructures.

Bug Reporting Process

1

Navigate Repository

Identify the specific repository where you encountered the bug.

2

Check Existing Issues

Review current issues to avoid reporting a duplicate bug.

3

Open New Issue

Navigate to the Issues tab and click "New issue" button.

4

Select Template

Choose the "Bug Report" template from available options.

5

Provide Details

Fill out the template with comprehensive bug information.

6

Submit Issue

Finalize and submit your bug report issue.

Provide clear, reproducible steps and include relevant environment details to help developers investigate effectively.
